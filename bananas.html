So the other day I was pondering bananas and wondered how many bananas could fit inside the Earth

There are about 155 million tons of bananas, but we'll actually need a few more than this. How many more?

Well, this should give you some idea: If we assume the Earth is a perfect sphere, (which it isn't and it also isn't flat) we can make a rough calculation.

Volume of Earth: The Earth has a radius of about 6,371 km, so its volume (using the volume of a sphere formula, V=43πr3V=34​πr3) is roughly:

VEarth≈43π(6,371,000 m)3≈1.083×1021 m3

VEarth​≈34​π(6,371,000m)3≈1.083×1021m3

Volume of a Banana: A typical banana has a volume of about 150 cm³ or 0.00015 m30.00015m3.

Number of Bananas: Dividing the Earth's volume by the volume of one banana:

1.083×1021 m30.00015 m3/banana≈7.22×1024 bananas

0.00015m3/banana1.083×1021m3​≈7.22×1024 bananas

15% of the Earth's total weight, assuming there’s no wasted space. I did all this math myself.

So yeah. Just a couple bananas.

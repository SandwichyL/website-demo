Understanding Euler's Formula
Euler's formula links exponential functions involving imaginary numbers to the unit circle in trigonometry, where the cosine and sine functions describe points on the circle.
Breaking Down the Formula Complex Exponential: The expression eiθeiθ represents an exponential function with an imaginary exponent. Using Euler's formula, we find that this expression is equivalent to a point on the unit circle.
Trigonometric Connection: The cosine and sine functions on the right side describe the x- and y-coordinates of a point on the unit circle for the angle θθ. This means that eiθeiθ essentially represents a point that lies on the unit circle at an angle θθ from the positive x-axis.
Interpreting Geometrically: Geometrically, if you start at 1+0i1+0i (the point (1, 0) on the complex plane) and rotate counterclockwise by an angle θθ, you reach the point cos⁡(θ)+isin⁡(θ)cos(θ)+isin(θ) on the unit circle.
